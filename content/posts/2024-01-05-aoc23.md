I really enjoyed Advent Of Code 2023 and I wanted to share my thoughs around it in this post.
I collected 40/50 stars this year which by far my most "succesful" season so far.
In this post, I will try to explain my approach to the problems and why that was the key factor to why it was the most succesful season so for. 
Beyond my approach to the problems I also want to share some of the stuff I learned along the way.

## But first, what even is Advent Of Code?

Advent of Code is an annual online programming competition created by Eric Wastl.
It takes place during the month of December, leading up to Christmas Day.
Each day, starting from December 1st, a new coding puzzle is released on the [Advent Of Code website](https://adventofcode.com/).

## My approach

This year I wanted to try a different approach to tackle the puzzles overall since for the previous seasons I have gotten demotivated around day 13 and this season I wanted to continue a bit longer.
That was exactly what happenden, I continued for the whole December this season.
The main reason for this is that I got a fresh start each day.
Regardless if I finished yesterdays puzzle or not, I tried solving todays puzzle only.
A new day meant a new puzzle and I could move a way from "failures", i.e unsolved puzzles.
If I finished a puzzle early, I could just go back to a previus unfinished puzzle and continue working on it but that was just a bonus, nothing "mandatory".
I timedbox myself to 1.5 hours before work in the morning and then 1-2 hours during the evening, not more.
I wanted to maximize the fun and the learning while timeboxing myself.

My approach is best described with an flow chart...

![image](https://github.com/mile95/aoc23/assets/8545435/69d442ce-fa8e-4cd4-bd09-6d09d6ee6c55)

As everyone, I started with handling the example input.
But as most of you know, solving for the example input does not mean that the solution will solve for the real input.
Supringsly often, this is the case and it is very sad every time this happens.

But what I've added to my approach this season was that I allowed my self to "cheat".
The "cheating" is shown as the _"Lookup (similar) solution online and use it for debugging!"_ box.
Since I wanted to maximize my learnings and the fun, I allowed my self to use other people solutions for debugging my own.
I think this was a great addition to my approach flow.
I went to `r/adventofcode` and filtered the soltions megathread with only python solutions and looked for approaches similar to mine.
This helped me getting a few stars and I defently learned alot.

1. I stayed motivated
2. I read and went through other peoples code and solution
3. I got exposed to different way of thinking and attacking the problems

For me this was a typical win-win.
But as you see, I restricted myself to only do this after I managed to solve for for the example input.

I will defently use the same approach next season!
